About Us

We are an interdisciplinary research and advocacy initiative focused on supporting the implementation of the California Racial Justice Act (Cal. Penal § 745, RJA).

Our work centers on providing rigorous, evidence-based support to public defenders and legal teams advancing RJA claims. We bring together expertise in law and social science to identify, analyze, and document racial bias in legal processes.

Our goal is simple: help ensure that the promise of the RJA is realized in practice.

Our Work

We provide direct research and analytical support for RJA cases in three key areas:

  1. Expert Witness Support

    We serve as expert witnesses in RJA cases, offering testimony on racial bias in language, decision-making, and legal processes. Our analyses translate complex social scientific and linguistic evidence into clear, legally relevant findings.

  2. Research & Evidence Development

    We compile and synthesize existing research on racial bias, language, and inequality to support legal arguments. This includes building accessible literature bases that public defenders can draw on in RJA claims.

  3. Quantitative & Statistical Analysis

    4. We conduct statistical analyses to identify racial disparities in charging, sentencing, and other legal outcomes. Our work helps establish patterns of bias that meet the evidentiary standards required under the RJA.

Across all of our work, we aim to make high-quality research usable, accessible, and impactful in real legal contexts.

Our Mission

Our mission is to support public defenders and legal advocates in advancing Racial Justice Act claims by providing rigorous, accessible, and court-ready research on racial bias in the legal system.

We are committed to:

  • Strengthening the evidentiary foundation of RJA cases.
  • Promoting the application of social science research in legal practice.
  • Supporting defense teams working to challenge racial injustice.
  • Bridging the gap between academic research and legal advocacy.

Our Vision

We envision a legal system where claims of racial bias are not dismissed as anecdotal, but are systematically demonstrated, rigorously supported, and taken seriously by the courts.

In this system:

  • Public defenders have access to high-quality research and expert support.
  • Racial disparities are clearly documented and legally actionable.
  • The burden of proving bias does not fall on individuals alone, but is supported by robust empirical evidence.
  • Laws like the RJA function as meaningful tools for accountability, not symbolic reforms.

Our Objectives

Support RJA Litigation

Provide expert testimony, research, and analysis to strengthen individual RJA cases. Help build long-term infrastructure for supporting RJA claims across jurisdictions.

Produce Statistical Evidence

Conduct rigorous quantitative analyses that identify and document racial disparities in legal outcomes.

Build Accessible Knowledge

Develop accessible summaries of social science literature for use by public defenders and legal teams.

Translate Research into Practice

Translate primary and secondary research findings into actionable policy recommendations for legislators and practitioners.

The Team

Tilden "Tilly" Brooks

J.D.-Ph.D. Student / Yale Law School, Stanford University

Tilly Brooks, from New Haven, Connecticut, is pursuing a PhD in linguistics at Stanford School of Humanities and Sciences. She is concurrently pursuing a JD at Yale Law School. Prior to attending Stanford, she received a BA in linguistics from Yale College, where she pursued Indo-European philology before uncovering an interest in action-based research and the relationship between language and law. Focusing both on the effects of law and policy decisions on marginalized linguistic communities and the application of linguistic theories, research methods, and tools to interpretive legal processes, Tilly researches what she calls “the law of language and the language of law.” In the long term, she aims to draw communities of legal scholars, linguists, and legal practitioners together with the common goals of advancing linguistic justice in the practice of law, and refining the use of linguistic evidence and tools for law and policy purposes.

Tanajia Moye-Green

Ph.D. Student / Stanford University

Tanajia Moye-Green is a sociology PhD student at Stanford University and a Knight-Hennessy Scholar. Her research examines the economic impact of incarceration on families, with a focus on how loved ones—particularly partners—navigate financial strain, fines and fees, and the broader challenges of supporting justice-impacted individuals. She is also interested in how the consequences of maternal incarceration differ from those of paternal incarceration in shaping child and family well-being. Tanajia holds an MSc in Criminal Justice and Penal Change from the University of Strathclyde and a B.A. in Sociology and Anthropology from Washington and Lee University. She has conducted research with the Vera Institute of Justice and the National Institute for Criminal Justice Reform, and she currently works with Dr Sarah Brayne. She is also a Fulbright Postgraduate Awardee, NSF GRFP Fellow, and Beinecke Scholar.
